Dear Tinder Users,

It has come to my attention that there has been a serious breach of security and privacy in our iOS, and android applications of Tinder. It is with my sincerest apologies that you have been subjected to our lack of encryption in using the app.

It was reported that our app versions were lacking in basic HTTPS encryptions which would have resulted in hackers being able to breach your privacy and ‘see’ the images of those you were swiping, profile pictures as well as swipe actions just by being allowed on the same network. We want to thank Checkmarx for pointing this issue out to us as they conducted their own test to assure us of the problem so that we could pass this onto our IT teams for the developments. Whilst we have released statements that our web-based application and other systems were heavily encrypted, it is still inexcusable that we had not sorted this encryption much earlier.

Since being notified of the breach, the letter we received from Senator Ron Wyden essentially assured the Senator that we have updated and fixed these breaches to avoid any potential blackmail or privacy issues moving forward. In that letter we highlighted that, “images were fully encrypted and swipe data was padded to give actions on the app the exact same data sizes”.

Rest assured that the breach has been dealt with but we apologise for any inconveniences for those users who may have already been subjected to blackmail and/or privacy breaches because of their use of the app prior to the fixes. If you were one of those users who experienced any of the issues mentioned we are offering free GOLD membership for 3 MONTHS enabling you to use our most prominent features and services.

We hope you all will still continue to use our services as we aim to always connect people!

Sincerely,
